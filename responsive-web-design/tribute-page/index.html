Nelson Mandela

The man who fought against all odds

Here's a timeline of Nelson Mandela's life:

  • 1918 - Born in Mvezo, Eastern Cape, South Africa
  • 1927 - His father passes away, leading to Mandela's move to Qunu under the guardianship of Chief ongintaba Dalindyebo​
  • 1930s - Attends Clarkebury Boarding Institute and Healdtown for primary education
  • 1941 - Moves to Johannesburg where he works as a night watchman and law clerk while completing his law degree at the University of Witwatersrand​
  • 1943 - Joins the African National Congress (ANC) and he becomes active in its Youth League
  • 1944 - Co-founds the ANC Youth League, advocating for radical strategies to fight apartheid​
  • 1952 - Leads the Defiance Campaign, the first large-scale, non-violent resistance against apartheid laws​
  • 1953 - Co-founds South Africa’s first Black law firm, Mandela & Tambo, where he offers affordable legal counsel to oppressed communities ​
  • 1961 - Helps to establish Umkhonto we Sizwe, an armed wing of the ANC, after peaceful protests proved ineffective
  • 1962 - He is arrested and sentenced to five years for inciting strikes and leaving the country without a passport
  • 1990 - He is released from prison after 27 years amid growing international and internal pressure to end apartheid​
  • 1991 - He becomes President-Elect of the ANC, leading negotiations for a democratic South Africa​
  • 1993 - He is awarded the Nobel Peace Prize alongside F.W. de Klerk for their work in fighting apartheid​
  • 1994 - Becomes South Africa’s first democratically elected Black president, emphasizing unity and reconciliation​
  • 1999 - Retires from politics but continues his humanitarian work in the fight against HIV/AIDS​
  • 2013 - Passes away at the age of 95
